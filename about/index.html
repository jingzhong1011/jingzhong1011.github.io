About Me

Hi, I'm Jing-Zhong Wang.

I am a biostatistician and epidemiologist interested in how population health changes across age, place and time, and how quantitative evidence can inform more thoughtful health policy. I hold a Master’s degree in Biostatistics and Health Data Science from National Taiwan University, with training in Epidemiology, Bayesian modeling, and population health analysis.

My work focuses on mortality and life expectancy analysis, small-area and spatiotemporal modeling, and the use of uncertainty-aware methods to support policy-relevant insights. Alongside a strong foundation in statistical modeling, I actively explore new methodologies and am particularly interested in machine learning approaches where they can complement and extend traditional epidemiological frameworks.
